BanglaQA: A Large-Scale Question Answering Benchmark for the Bengali Language

Tahsin Hossain , Fatema Begum , Lamia Chowdhury , Md. Anisur Rahman (BUET)

We introduce BanglaQA, a extractive and abstractive question answering dataset containing 12,400 QA pairs across eight domains including law, healthcare, and social science. We establish strong baselines using multilingual BERT variants and a novel Bengali-specific pre-trained encoder, demonstrating significant gaps relative to English QA performance that motivate further low-resource NLP research.

NLP Low-Resource Bengali QA

CampusEye: Anchor-Free Multi-Object Tracking for Resource-Constrained Edge Deployment

Arif Rahman , Mehedi Hasan , Sadia Akter

We present CampusEye, a lightweight multi-object tracking framework built on an anchor-free YOLOv8 backbone with a novel re-identification head optimised for constrained hardware. On the MOT17 benchmark, our model achieves 68.4 HOTA while running at 42 FPS on a Jetson Orin Nano, outperforming comparable methods by 4.2 HOTA points at half the latency.

Computer Vision Object Tracking Edge AI

Stylistic Latent Diffusion for Regional Folk Art Generation with Cultural Fidelity Metrics

Nadia Islam , Sadia Akter , Fatema Begum

We propose a fine-tuning methodology for latent diffusion models that preserves the stylistic integrity of culturally significant art traditions. Applied to Bangladeshi Nakshi Kantha and Jamdani motifs, our approach introduces a Cultural Fidelity Score (CFS) metric and demonstrates that LoRA-based fine-tuning on curated datasets of 2,400 images achieves superior CLIP-based style alignment over full fine-tuning baselines.

Computer Vision Generative AI Diffusion Models LoRA

SentimentBD: A Benchmark for Aspect-Level Sentiment Analysis in Code-Mixed Bengali–English

Lamia Chowdhury , Fatema Begum , Shafiq Joty (NTU)

Code-mixing in Bengali social media presents unique challenges for sentiment analysis systems trained on monolingual data. We release SentimentBD — 50,400 annotated posts from Facebook and Twitter with fine-grained aspect and polarity labels — and show that a language-adaptive pre-training strategy on code-mixed text reduces cross-lingual transfer error by 19% over mBERT baselines.

NLP Sentiment Analysis Code-Mixing Bengali

Temporal Fusion Transformers for Campus-Scale Energy Demand Forecasting with Uncertainty Quantification

Raihan Kabir , Fatema Begum , Dr. Kamrul Hasan (KUET)

Accurate energy demand forecasting is critical for smart campus infrastructure. We apply Temporal Fusion Transformers (TFT) to 30 years of electricity consumption data from a Bangladeshi university, incorporating weather, occupancy, and calendar features. Our model achieves a 18% reduction in peak load prediction error versus LSTM baselines, with calibrated prediction intervals enabling risk-aware demand response.

Data Science Time Series Forecasting Energy

Domain Randomisation Strategies for Robust Sim-to-Real Transfer in Indoor Mobile Robot Navigation

Tahsin Hossain , Raihan Kabir , Prof. Mahbub Hassan (UNSW)

Transferring policies trained in simulation to real robots remains a key bottleneck in practical RL-based robotics. We systematically evaluate nine domain randomisation strategies — spanning dynamics, visual appearance, and sensor noise — for a Soft Actor-Critic agent navigating cluttered indoor environments. A structured randomisation curriculum reduces sim-to-real performance gap by 31% compared to uniform randomisation, achieving 89% navigation success on unseen floor layouts.

Reinforcement Learning Robotics Sim-to-Real SAC

BioNER-BD: A Named Entity Recognition Corpus for Bangla Clinical and Biomedical Text

Fatema Begum , Tahsin Hossain , Dr. Farhana Sarker (BSMMU)

Clinical NLP for Bangla is severely under-resourced. We present BioNER-BD, an IRB-approved annotated corpus of 8,200 sentences from de-identified electronic health records covering disease, symptom, drug, and procedure entities. Fine-tuning BioBERT with a Bangla-adapted tokenizer achieves 81.3 F1 on our test split, establishing the first strong baseline for Bangla clinical NER.

NLP Biomedical NER Bengali

Efficient Fine-Tuning of Vision Transformers for Flood Mapping from Low-Resolution Satellite Imagery

Arif Rahman , Nadia Islam , SPARRSO Collaboration

Rapid and accurate flood mapping from satellite imagery is essential for disaster response in Bangladesh, one of the world's most flood-prone countries. We fine-tune a DINOv2-based segmentation head on Sentinel-1 SAR imagery using parameter-efficient adapters, achieving 87.2% mIoU on a new Bangladesh Flood Segmentation Dataset (BFSD) we release with 1,240 annotated scenes.

Computer Vision Remote Sensing Vision Transformers Segmentation
